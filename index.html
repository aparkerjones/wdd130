Idaho

Idaho is a state in the Pacific Northwest known for its rugged natural beauty and abundant outdoor recreation opportunities. Nicknamed the "Gem State" due to its rich mineral deposits, Idaho is dominated by the Rocky Mountains, which contribute to its dramatic landscapes of mountains, rivers, lakes, and Hells Canyon, the deepest river gorge in North America. Idaho's economy has a strong foundation in agriculture—it produces nearly one-third of the nation's potatoes—but also relies heavily on manufacturing, technology, tourism, and forestry. The capital and largest city is Boise, which has grown into a hub for technology and culture, balancing the state's otherwise rural character and strong frontier heritage
